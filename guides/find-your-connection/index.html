How to find your connection type

You need this for the connection calculator. It's printed right on your equipment — you just need to know where to look. Takes about 2 minutes.

1. Find your meter cupboard

Dutch homes call this the meterkast. It's usually a narrow cupboard near the front door, in the hallway, or in the garage/basement of older homes. You're looking for a grey or white metal box containing your smart meter and a row of switches.

2. Count the fuses in the main fuse block

Look near the top of the cupboard for a sealed block (hoofdzekering) — don't touch the seal, but you can read it. Count how many fuse holders are bundled together:

One fuse = single-phase. Three identical fuses bundled together = three-phase.

3. Read the amperage

The rating is printed on the fuse holder itself — look for 25A, 35A or 50A.

You see Your connection
One fuse, "25A" 1x25A
One fuse, "35A" 1x35A
Three fuses, "25A" each 3x25A
Three fuses, "35A" each 3x35A
Three fuses, "50A" each 3x50A

Can't find it or it's not labelled?

  • Check your annual bill (jaarafrekening) — look for aansluitwaarde
  • Contact your regional grid operator (Liander, Stedin, Enexis, Rendo or Coteq) — they can tell you using your address
